# HealthNet

HealthNet is a web-based platform that helps public health agencies, hospitals, and research institutions work together to keep communities healthy. It brings citizens, doctors, laboratories, researchers, and health officials onto a single system so that disease outbreaks can be spotted early, patient care stays organized, and public health decisions are based on real, up-to-date information.

## What HealthNet Does

In simple terms, HealthNet is a digital hub for everything that happens around public health. It lets:

- **Citizens** report symptoms, receive health alerts, and view easy-to-understand dashboards about their wellbeing and the health of their community.
- **Doctors** record patient cases, prescribe treatments, and keep an eye on diseases that are spreading.
- **Lab Technicians** upload test results and diagnostic reports so doctors and officials get the information they need quickly.
- **Public Health Officers** monitor outbreaks, check that health rules are being followed, and generate reports for decision-makers.
- **Researchers** study disease patterns and turn raw data into insights that help prevent future health problems.
- **Administrators** manage users, set up workflows, and make sure the platform runs smoothly.
- **Compliance Officers** review records and confirm that everyone is following health policies and regulations.

## Why It Matters

Public health work involves a lot of moving parts — patient visits, lab tests, outbreak alerts, policy checks, and reports for governments and the public. When this information lives in scattered spreadsheets, paper files, or separate systems, important signals get missed and responses get slower.

HealthNet keeps everything in one place. That means:

- Outbreaks can be noticed and acted on earlier.
- Patients get faster, better-informed care.
- Officials can see the big picture across hospitals, labs, and regions.
- Every action is recorded, so audits and compliance checks are simple and transparent.
- Researchers can study trends without chasing data from a dozen different sources.

## Key Workflows

HealthNet supports the everyday tasks that public health teams rely on:

- Registering and updating patient information
- Logging disease cases and treatments
- Recording and sharing laboratory test results
- Watching for and responding to outbreaks
- Tracking compliance with health rules and policies
- Producing dashboards and reports for leaders and the public
